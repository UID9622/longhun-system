# Mulan Protocol v1.0

**木兰协议 v1.0 (Chinese Version Available)**

## Preamble

This protocol aims to establish an open, transparent, and sustainable technology cooperation ecosystem. Through innovative governance structures and value-alignment mechanisms, it ensures that all participants achieve win-win outcomes in technological development and commercial success.

This protocol is based on the following core values:
- Open source technology sharing
- Value DNA alignment
- God's Eye full supervision
- Fair and reasonable equity structure
- One-vote veto power guarantee mechanism

---

## Chapter 1: Definitions

### 1.1 Core Term Definitions

**God's Eye**: Refers to an independent third-party supervision mechanism that monitors protocol execution through technical means, ensuring transparency and fairness.

**Value DNA Alignment**: Refers to the requirement for parties to align with the protocol's core values in terms of technology philosophy, ethical standards, and social responsibilities.

**One-Vote Veto Power**: Refers to the final decision-making power retained by the protocol founder on major matters, ensuring core values are not compromised.

**Partners Hold 51% but Don't Manage**: Refers to partners holding 51% equity stake but not participating in daily operational decisions, only enjoying profit distribution rights.

**Technology Achievements Open Source Sharing**: Refers to all technical outcomes produced during protocol execution being made available to society through open source methods.

### 1.2 Legal Term Definitions

**Signatory**: Refers to individuals or organizations who sign and commit to comply with this protocol.

**Protocol Amendment**: Refers to modifications and updates to this protocol's terms through formal procedures.

**Breach of Contract**: Refers to acts that violate the provisions of this protocol.

**Arbitration Mechanism**: Refers to procedures and methods for resolving disputes through independent third parties.

## Chapter 2: Rights and Obligations

### 2.1 Founder's Rights

**2.1.1 One-Vote Veto Power**
The founder retains one-vote veto power over the following matters:
- Changes to core values
- Modifications to basic protocol principles
- Decisions affecting open source sharing mechanisms
- Matters contrary to God's Eye supervision principles

**2.1.2 Technology Direction Guidance Rights**
The founder has the right to:
- Determine technology development direction
- Establish technical standards and specifications
- Supervise technology achievement quality

### 2.2 Partner Rights

**2.2.1 Equity Profit Rights**
Partners are entitled to:
- 51% equity profit distribution
- Right to information on regular financial reports
- Right to suggest profit distribution plans

**2.2.2 Technology Usage Rights**
Partners have the right to:
- Freely use all open source technology achievements
- Participate in technology improvement and optimization
- Receive technical support and training

### 2.3 Common Obligations

**2.3.1 Open Source Sharing Obligations**
All signatories must:
- Open source technology achievements promptly
- Comply with open source license requirements
- Promote technology community development

**2.3.2 Value Alignment Obligations**
All signatories must:
- Maintain core values
- Participate in value DNA assessment
- Cooperate with God's Eye supervision

## Chapter 3: Equity Structure and Governance

### 3.1 Equity Distribution Principles

**3.1.1 Partners Hold 51% but Don't Manage**
- Partners hold 51% equity with profit rights but no operational participation

**3.1.2 Governance Mechanism**

- Technology open source compliance checks
- Value execution assessment

- Regular audit reports
- Public transparent data display
- Third-party independent verification

## Chapter 6: Protocol Amendment Mechanism

### 6.1 Version Management Principles

**6.1.1 Semantic Version Control**
This protocol adopts semantic version control (SemVer):
- Major version number (v1.0 → v2.0): major changes
- Minor version number (v1.0 → v1.1): feature additions/deletions
- Patch number (v1.0.1): bug fixes

**6.1.2 Amendment Thresholds**
- Major version change: requires unanimous consent of all signatories
- Minor version change: requires two-thirds consent of signatories
- Patch change: requires founder's approval

### 6.2 Amendment Process

**6.2.1 Proposal Stage**
1. Any signatory may propose amendments
2. Proposals must include specific amendment content, reasons, and impact analysis
3. After submission, proposals enter a 30-day community discussion period

**6.2.2 Discussion Stage**
1. Discuss on open platforms
2. Collect opinions and suggestions from all parties
3. Make necessary improvements
4. Form final amendment plan

**6.2.3 Voting Stage**
1. Determine voting threshold based on amendment type
2. Use blockchain technology to ensure transparent and fair voting
3. Publish voting results instantly with evidence storage

**6.2.4 Effective Stage**
1. Automatically becomes effective after reaching consent threshold
2. Signatories disagreeing with new version retain rights under original protocol
3. Coexistence of new and old versions ensures cooperation continuity

### 6.3 Backward Compatibility Mechanism

**6.3.1 Version Compatibility Principles**
- New protocol versions do not automatically override old version signatories
- Signatories can choose to upgrade or maintain original protocol
- Ensure cooperation continuity is not affected by protocol amendments

**6.3.2 Upgrade Mechanism**
- Provide clear upgrade paths and timelines
- Establish version migration technical support
- Ensure data integrity during upgrade process
